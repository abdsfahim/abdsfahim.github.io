Who I Am

About Me

I'm a final-year Electrical & Electronic Engineering student at Jashore University of Science and Technology (JUST), Bangladesh, with a CGPA of 3.81/4.00 — consistently improving each semester from 3.53 to near-perfect 3.99.

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning and physical systems — building surrogate models that replace computationally expensive simulations, and designing end-to-end AI-powered health monitoring systems.

Beyond research, I served as Chair of IEEE JUST Student Branch, where I led strategic initiatives, organized international conferences, and mentored the next generation of engineers.

I am actively preparing to pursue a fully-funded PhD for Fall 2027 in the USA or Europe.

What I Study

Research Interests

⚛️
Semiconductor Process ML
Building ML surrogate models for ion implantation simulation in Si, SiC, and GaAs — replacing slow Monte Carlo tools with millisecond inference at R² > 0.98 accuracy.
🫀
Biomedical & Predictive Healthcare
Designing IoT-enabled non-invasive systems that measure biomarkers and predict cardiovascular disease risk using Gradient Boosting — 89% accuracy on the Framingham Dataset.
🔬
Computational Material Science
Using SRIM/TRIM simulations and physics-informed ML to model ion transport, range, straggle, and vacancy production across wide-bandgap semiconductor substrates.
🔌
VLSI & Embedded Systems
Hands-on experience with analog IC design, custom layout, and place-and-route. Embedded systems development using Arduino, ESP32, and real-time PID control.
